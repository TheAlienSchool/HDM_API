# Magnetism in Plain English: An Annotated Bibliography

This bibliography provides a multi-disciplinary foundation for the principles and practices outlined in *Magnetism in Plain English*. It weaves together scientific research, ancient wisdom traditions, modern sage insights, and poetic voices to create a rich tapestry of validation for the book’s core teachings on relational living and vibrational awareness.

## Part I: Scientific Foundations & Experimental Evidence

This section grounds the principles of magnetism in empirical research from physics, neuroscience, biology, and systems theory, demonstrating that the relational worldview is not only a philosophical stance but also an experimentally verifiable reality.

### 1. Resonance, Vibration, and Consciousness

The core of magnetism is the understanding that everything is in a state of vibration. Modern science is beginning to recognize resonance as fundamental to consciousness itself, bridging the gap between the material and the experiential.

| Author(s) | Work | Key Contribution |
|---|---|---|
| **Hunt, T., & Schooler, J. W.** | "The Easy Part of the Hard Problem: A Resonance Theory of Consciousness" (2019) | Proposes that all consciousness is a product of resonance, where synchronized vibrations lead to the emergence of larger, more complex conscious entities from smaller ones. This provides a direct scientific framework for understanding how shared resonance creates connection and expands awareness [1]. |
| **Sheldrake, R.** | *Morphic Resonance: The Nature of Formative Causation* (2009) | Argues that memory is inherent in nature and that similar patterns of vibration (morphic fields) influence subsequent patterns across time and space, providing a scientific basis for collective memory and the transmission of habits and forms [2]. |
| **Bohm, D.** | *Wholeness and the Implicate Order* (1980) | A foundational work in theoretical physics that posits an "implicate order"—an undivided, underlying wholeness from which our manifest reality (the "explicate order") unfolds. This supports the teaching that separation is an illusion and that the whole is enfolded in every part [3]. |
| **Various** | *Cymatics* | The study of visible sound and vibration, which demonstrates how specific frequencies create consistent, complex geometric patterns in matter. This provides a visual, tangible proof that vibration creates form, validating the creative power of resonance [4]. |

> "If you want to find the secrets of the universe, think in terms of energy, frequency and vibration." 
> — **Nikola Tesla**

### 2. Interconnection and Non-Locality

Magnetism teaches that we are not separate individuals but nodes in a vast web of interconnection. Research in quantum physics and human physiology provides compelling evidence for this ancient wisdom.

| Author(s) | Work | Key Contribution |
|---|---|---|
| **Various** | *Quantum Entanglement* | The principle that particles of a common origin remain instantaneously connected, influencing each other regardless of the distance separating them. This "spooky action at a distance" (Einstein) provides a scientific basis for non-local connection and the fundamental interconnectedness of the universe [5]. |
| **McCraty, R., et al. (HeartMath Institute)** | "The Coherent Heart: Heart-Brain Interactions, Psychophysiological Coherence, and the Emergence of System-Wide Order" (2009) | Demonstrates that the human heart generates a powerful electromagnetic field (5,000 times stronger than the brain's) that radiates outside the body. When an individual is in a state of heart coherence, this field becomes more organized and can be detected by others nearby, providing a physical mechanism for energetic communication and resonance [6]. |
| **Capra, F.** | *The Web of Life: A New Scientific Understanding of Living Systems* (1996) | A landmark work in systems theory that describes all living systems as interconnected networks of relationships. Capra provides a scientific language for understanding the interdependence of all phenomena, from the biological to the social and ecological [7]. |

## Part II: Ancient Wisdom & Diasporic Traditions

The principles of magnetism are not new; they are ancient truths that have been preserved in wisdom traditions across the globe. This section honors the diverse cultural expressions of relational living.

### 3. African & Indigenous Philosophies of Interconnection

These traditions offer profound, time-tested frameworks for understanding the self as fundamentally relational and inseparable from the community and the land.

| Tradition | Core Concept | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Ubuntu (Southern Africa)** | *"I am because we are."* | A foundational philosophy emphasizing that a person's humanity is inextricably bound to the humanity of others. It promotes a worldview of communalism, shared responsibility, and radical interconnectedness, as articulated by figures like Desmond Tutu [8]. |
| **"All My Relations" (Indigenous North America)** | *Mitakuye Oyasin* | A sacred phrase and worldview that recognizes all beings—human, animal, plant, and mineral—as relatives in a vast, interconnected family. It is a declaration of unity and a call to live in right relationship with all of creation [9]. |
| **Aboriginal Songlines (Australia)** | *Dreaming Tracks* | Ancient pathways of knowledge that crisscross the land, sky, and water, encoding ancestral stories, laws, and ecological wisdom. These songlines are living maps of resonance, connecting people to their country and to the Dreamtime, the all-at-once time of creation [10]. |

### 4. Eastern & Esoteric Philosophies of Oneness

From the intricate metaphors of Buddhism to the mystical axioms of Hermeticism, these traditions provide rich philosophical maps of the unified reality that magnetism invites us to experience.

| Tradition | Core Concept | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Buddhism** | *Interbeing & Indra's Net* | Thich Nhat Hanh's concept of "interbeing" is a modern expression of the ancient Buddhist teaching of Dependent Origination—the idea that nothing exists independently. This is beautifully illustrated by the metaphor of Indra's Net, a vast cosmic web with a jewel at each intersection that reflects all the other jewels, symbolizing the infinite interconnectedness of all phenomena [11]. |
| **Sufism (Rumi)** | *The Unity of Being* | The mystical poetry of Rumi constantly points to the dissolution of the separate self into the ocean of divine unity. His work is a passionate expression of the longing for and experience of oneness, where the lover, beloved, and love itself merge into a single reality [12]. |
| **Hermeticism** | *"As Above, So Below"* | This ancient axiom, found in the Emerald Tablet, encapsulates the Principle of Correspondence—the idea that there is a harmony and correspondence between the different planes of existence (physical, mental, and spiritual). It suggests that the patterns of the cosmos are reflected in the human being, and vice versa. The Principle of Vibration ("Nothing rests; everything moves; everything vibrates") is another core Hermetic teaching that directly aligns with magnetism [13]. |

## Part III: Modern Sages & Poetic Voices

This section includes contemporary thinkers and artists whose work continues to explore and articulate the principles of magnetism for a modern audience.

> "Tell me about despair, yours, and I will tell you mine. 
> Meanwhile the world goes on. 
> Meanwhile the sun and the clear pebbles of the rain 
> are moving across the landscapes, 
> over the prairies and the deep trees, 
> the mountains and the rivers. 
> Meanwhile the wild geese, high in the clean blue air, 
> are heading home again. 
> Whoever you are, no matter how lonely, 
> the world offers itself to your imagination, 
> calls to you like the wild geese, harsh and exciting— 
> over and over announcing your place 
> in the family of things." 
> — **Mary Oliver**, "Wild Geese" [14]

| Author | Key Contribution |
|---|---|
| **Audre Lorde** | In her essays, particularly "The Master's Tools Will Never Dismantle the Master's House," Lorde speaks to the power of interdependence, especially among those in marginalized communities. She argues that our differences, when acknowledged and embraced, are a source of creative power and that true freedom lies in mutual, non-dominant relationships [15]. |
| **Bruce Lipton** | In *The Biology of Belief*, Lipton, a cell biologist, demonstrates how our beliefs and perceptions directly influence our genetic expression (epigenetics). His work provides a scientific basis for understanding how consciousness and environment shape our biology, validating the power of our internal state to influence our physical reality. |
| **Lynne McTaggart** | In *The Field*, McTaggart synthesizes a vast body of scientific research on the Zero Point Field, a quantum field of infinite potential that connects everything in the universe. Her work provides a scientific framework for understanding phenomena like remote healing, telepathy, and the power of intention. |
